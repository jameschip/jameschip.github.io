A Wood Heart

{ September 2020 in*die Zine on Itch }

A Wood Heart is a game that I originally produced for in*die zines Setptember 2020 release but later expanded into afuller game in early 2021.

It was written with 100 open source software and layed out in LaTeX. The physical zine is printed on recyled paper.

The Game

A wood Heart is a map drawing and story telling game about trees, people and spirits for 2-4 players; it takes about an hour to play.

It is a game about human settlement and the impact we, as a species, have on the world around us.

To play you will need a large sheet of paper, some pencils, a six sided die and a deck of tarot cards.

The Forest

At the start of the game you will be creating a forest and the spirits that live in it.

The forest you create is filled with flora, fauna and landmarks that are unique to it alone. These are the things that, to the casual observer, seem perfectly ordinary; but to your forest they are the core of its identity.

At the centre of this forest is A Wood Heart, the beating heart of the forest that gives it life. Collectively you will describe the Wood Heart that sits at the centre of your forest; then draw a map of your forest and the landmarks within it.

The Spirits

During the game you will be playing the roles of the spirits, coming to the Wood Heart to tell stories about what the humans have been doing.

Discover fragments of the spirits pasts and then use tarot cards and the given prompts to discover what events happen and what stories the spirits tell to the Wood Heart.

These spirits need a thriving forest to keep life sustainable for them. Will your forest thrive and be able to support these spirits or will they be forced to leave and find life elsewhere?

The Humans

New to the area, humans, have set up a small settlement on the edge of the forest. At first everything was fine, but then they started to take more from the land than they could give.

Over time this settlement may grow and change, affecting the world around it as it does. Overtime the humans may take things from The Forest for their own gain.

Will the actions of the humans destroy The Forest forever? Can your forest defend itself and slowly drive the human threat away?
